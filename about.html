The Origin

It started with one bad
keke ride too many.

Transport in Ile-Ife has always been chaotic. Students waiting 30 minutes on Obafemi Awolowo University's main road. Residents haggling with drivers who quoted different prices to different faces. No accountability. No consistency. No safety.

The founders of Rydex lived this problem personally — as students, as residents, as people who simply needed to get somewhere and couldn't. They didn't just complain. They built a solution specifically tuned to this city, this university, and this community.

Unlike generic ride-hailing platforms that treat every city the same, Rydex was designed from the ground up for Ile-Ife. Our pricing reflects local realities. Our driver network knows these streets. Our technology is lightweight enough to work on any device.

We launched quietly, with a handful of drivers and a simple app. Word spread fast — because when something actually works, people talk.

2024 — Q1

The idea takes shape

After one too many failed attempts to find a reliable ride on campus, the founding team begins researching what a locally-built solution could look like.

2024 — Q3

First drivers onboarded

A small fleet of vetted drivers goes live. Early riders are students and staff within OAU. The feedback is immediate and overwhelming: this is what we needed.

2025

Expanding across Ile-Ife

Coverage grows beyond campus into Ile-Ife town centre, Lagere, Mayfair, and surrounding areas. The platform scales to serve the full city.
